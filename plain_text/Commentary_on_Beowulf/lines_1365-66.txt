Although the mysterious file may be nothing but the will-o'-the-wisp, it is worth noting that "the burning lake or river . . . is one of the commonest features of all, Oriental as well as Christian, accounts of hell" (E. Becker, The Medieval Visions of Heaven and Hell [Johns Hopkins Diss., 1899], p. 37); cf. Angl. xxxvi 186.--The subject (indef. pronoun man) is lefi unexpressed, just as 'he' in 1367b. Cf Lang  § 25.4.
