For [g]ewy[r]htum is parallel to for ārstafum (for denoting cause, not purpose); 'because of deeds done' (ref to the good services tendered to Bēowulf's father, 463 ff.) -- and 'the resultant obligations you are under.' Accordingly, the meaning of 457 f. is: 'from a sense of duty and kindness you have come to us.' (JEGPh. vi 191 f.) [Cf. also Siev. ix 138, xxxvi 401 f.; Bu. 87 f.; Aant. 9 f.; Tr.1 152 f.; Holt.
