nō hīe fæder cunnon, 'they have no knowledge of a father.' The meaning of hwæþer him ǣnig wæs ǣr ācenned/dyrnra gāsta is brought out in Earle's rendering, "whether they [i.e., the two demons] had any in pedigree before them of mysterious goblins " ; with ǣr, 'previously' (prior to them), cp. æfter 12, 2731. It is of interest to note that the Danes know less than the poet (see 106 ff., 1261 ff.).
