onband beadūrine, 'unbound a battle-rune,' i.e. 'disclosed a hidden quarrel' (see note on eardlufan 692), 'began a bellicose speech.' It is probable that only the vaguest suggestion of ancient heathen belief (Müllenhoff in R. v. Liliencron & K. Müllenhoff, Zur Runenlehre [1852], p. 44) was lingering in beadurūn. Cp. El. 28: wælrūne ne māð, 1098: hygerūne ne māð. The use of onbindan is illustrated by Beow. 259, 489.
