swanrād. Cp. hronrād 10, ganotes bæð 1861. According to the Encycloþædia Britannica 11, xxvi 179 f., the (mute or tame) swan (cygnus olor) "is known to breed as a wild bird not farther from the British shores than the extreme south of Sweden." The whooper, whistling or wild swan (cygnus musicus) "was doubtless always a winter-visitant to Britain, . . . . . it is a native of Iceland, eastern Lapland, and northern Russia, whence it wanders southward in autumn." -- See the 8th Riddle.
