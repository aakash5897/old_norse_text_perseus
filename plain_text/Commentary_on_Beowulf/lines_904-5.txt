Hine sorhwylmas/lemede tō lange. Heremōd was unhappy during the greater part of his life (tö lange); first because excluded from the throne and exiled, later because hated by his own people and put to death. The singular of the verb may be explained syntactically, sorhbwylmas being felt to be equal to sorb. Cf. Lang.  § § 25.6, 19.3; also Dietrich, Zfd A. x 332 f., xi 444 ff. Only sporadically do we find the ending -on of the pret. ind. plur. of wk. verbs weakened to -e; cf. E. M. Brown, The Lang. of the Rushbw. Gloss to Matthew, ii (1892),  § 38; O. Eger, Dialekt. in den Flexionsverhaltnissen der ags. Bedaübersetzung (Leipzig Diss., 1910),  § 13.
