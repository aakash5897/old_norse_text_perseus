þæt se hearmscaþa tō Heorute ātēah. Kock2 106 ff. argues for the relative character of this clause, pæt (instead of pone) being justified by pæt 765; sīð ātēon, 'take a journey.' Cp. 1455 f. This is indeed more satisfactory than to take pæt as conjunct. and ātēon as intrans. verb (as suggested MPh. iii 455).
