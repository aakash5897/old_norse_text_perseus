On (see Gloss.; Lang.  § 25.5) may be translated by 'from,' but the underlying syntactical conception is not that of motion, on ræste belonging in fact with the object of the verb (cp. 747, 1298, 1302); see note on 575.--Of the disposal of the thirty men we are told in 1580 ff.
