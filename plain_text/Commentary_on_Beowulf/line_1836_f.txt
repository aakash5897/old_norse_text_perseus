Gif him þonne Hrēþrīc tō hofum Gēata/geþingeð, '. . . determines [to go] to . . .' Exact parallels of this function of (refl.) geþingan occur Bi Domes D. 5, Sat. 598 (cf. Aant. 28). For the omission of the verb of motion, see Gloss.: willan, sculan; Ælfric's Saints xxvi 213; þider hē gemynt hæfde; also La[yogh  ]amon's Brut 28109: þā þū tō Rōme þohtest; etc. The meaning ' (arrange to) take service' has been conjectured for geþingan (Ger. 'sich verdingen,' cf. Heyne-Schücking, Lorz 68), but this is not well attested.
