 On the beheading of Grendel, see Intr. xviii; Panzer 288 f. To an unprejudiced reader it may seem natural enough that the head of Grendel, the chief of the enemies, is cut off and carried home in triumph. But, as an additional leason, the desire of preventing the ghost from haunting Heorot has been cited (see Gering's note). 1590b. ond . . þā, 'and thus (so)' ; cp. 2707.
