þǣr unc hwïle wæs hand gemǣne. "There to us for a while was the blendingof hands" (W. Morris), or . . . "battle joined" (Sedgefield). Cp. 2473; Wulfst. 162.7 f.: þæt wǣpengeewrixl weorðe gemǣne þegene and þrǣle. The Ger. handgemein (werden) furnishes a semasiological, though not a syntactical parallel.
