Geseah ðā on searwum sigeēadig bil. Several translations of on searwum seem possible; viz. 'among [other] arms' (see 1613), 'in battle' ('during the fight'), ' [he] in his armor' (cp. 2568), or (construing the prepositional phrase with bil) 'fully equipped,' ' ready' (cp. fūslīc, geatolīc). Probability is divided between the first and the last one.
