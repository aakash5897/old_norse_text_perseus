incge-lāfe (perhaps a compound). incge is as obscure as icge 1107, with which (as well as with īsig 33) it has been conjecturally connected [Note also Ex. 190: inge men, 444: inca ðēode.] Inges, or Ingwines (see Proper Names, Intr. xxxvii, and note on Scyld, p. 123), is a desperate remedy for a desperate case. ē（a）cnan, īcnan, or īcnen (cp. 1663a, 2140a, 1104b [MS.]) could also be proposed. Quite possibly the scribe did not understand the word.
